# Topic 4: Work, Energy, and Power

## Overview
Energy is a fundamental concept in physics that simplifies solving mechanical problems. This topic covers work, kinetic and potential energy, conservation of energy, and power.

## Key Concepts

### 4.1 Work

**Definition:** Work is the transfer of energy by a force acting over a displacement.

$$W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{s} = Fs\cos\theta$$

where θ is the angle between force and displacement.

**Units:** Joule (J) = N·m = kg·m²/s²

**Work done by various forces:**
- By gravity: $W_g = mg(h_i - h_f)$ (negative if object moves up)
- By friction: $W_f = -\mu_k N \cdot d$ (always negative, opposes motion)
- By constant force at angle θ: $W = F \cos\theta \cdot d$

**Important:** Work is zero if:
- Force is perpendicular to displacement (θ = 90°)
- No displacement occurs (d = 0)
- Force and displacement are in opposite directions (θ = 180°)

### 4.2 Kinetic Energy

**Definition:** Energy due to motion.

$$KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$$

**Work-Energy Theorem:**
$$W_{net} = \Delta KE = KE_f - KE_i = \frac{1}{2}m(v_f^2 - v_i^2)$$

The net work done equals the change in kinetic energy.

### 4.3 Potential Energy

**Gravitational Potential Energy:**
$$PE_g = mgh$$

where h is height above reference level.

**Elastic Potential Energy (spring):**
$$PE_s = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$$

where k is spring constant and x is displacement from equilibrium.

**Conservative forces:** Work done is path-independent, depends only on initial and final positions.
- Gravity, spring forces
- Associated with potential energy

**Non-conservative forces:** Work depends on path.
- Friction, air resistance
- Dissipate mechanical energy as heat

### 4.4 Conservation of Mechanical Energy

For a system with only conservative forces:

$$E_{total} = KE + PE = \text{constant}$$

$$KE_i + PE_i = KE_f + PE_f$$

$$\frac{1}{2}mv_i^2 + mgh_i = \frac{1}{2}mv_f^2 + mgh_f$$

**With friction or non-conservative forces:**
$$E_i = E_f + W_{friction}$$

The "lost" mechanical energy is converted to heat.

### 4.5 Power

**Definition:** Rate of energy transfer or rate of doing work.

$$P = \frac{W}{t} = \frac{dE}{dt}$$

**Instantaneous power:**
$$P = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{v} = Fv\cos\theta$$

**Units:** Watt (W) = J/s

**Common powers:**
- Human walking: ~100 W
- Human climbing stairs: ~1000 W
- Car engine: ~50,000-100,000 W
- Large power plant: ~10⁹ W

**Energy equivalent:** 1 kWh = 3.6 × 10⁶ J

### 4.6 Collisions

**Elastic Collision:** Kinetic energy is conserved.
$$KE_{before} = KE_{after}$$

For 1D collision of two objects:
$$m_1 v_1 + m_2 v_2 = m_1 v_1' + m_2 v_2'$$ (momentum conservation)
$$\frac{1}{2}m_1 v_1^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_2 v_2^2 = \frac{1}{2}m_1 v_1'^2 + \frac{1}{2}m_2 v_2'^2$$ (energy conservation)

**Inelastic Collision:** Kinetic energy is not conserved (converted to heat, deformation).
Only momentum is conserved.

**Coefficient of Restitution:**
$$e = \frac{|v_2' - v_1'|}{|v_1 - v_2|}$$

- e = 1: perfectly elastic
- 0 < e < 1: partially inelastic
- e = 0: perfectly inelastic (objects stick together)

## Key Equations (Summary)

$$W = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{s} = Fs\cos\theta$$

$$W_{net} = \Delta KE = \frac{1}{2}m(v_f^2 - v_i^2)$$

$$PE_g = mgh, \quad PE_s = \frac{1}{2}kx^2$$

$$E_{total} = KE + PE = \text{constant}$$ (conservative forces)

$$P = \frac{W}{t} = \vec{F} \cdot \vec{v}$$

$$p = m\vec{v}$$ (momentum)

$$p_{before} = p_{after}$$ (momentum conservation)
