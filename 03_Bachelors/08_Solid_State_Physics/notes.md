# Solid State Physics: From Atoms to Devices

## Overview
Understanding solid materials: crystal structure, electron behavior in lattices, semiconductors, superconductors. Foundation of modern electronics and technology.

---

## 1. Crystal Structure

**Bravais lattice**: Infinite, periodic arrangement of equivalent points.

**Unit cell**: Smallest repeating unit containing full lattice information.

**Cubic systems**: Simple cubic, body-centered (BCC), face-centered (FCC).

**Miller indices** (hkl): Notation for crystal planes.

---

## 2. X-Ray Diffraction

**Bragg's law**:
$$n\lambda = 2d\sin\theta$$

where d = plane spacing, θ = incident angle.

**Powder diffraction**: Random crystal orientations → ring pattern at specific angles.

**Structure factor** determines which reflections are allowed.

---

## 3. Free Electron Model

**Drude model** (classical): electrons as gas with constant relaxation time τ.
$$\sigma = \frac{ne^2\tau}{m}$$ (conductivity)

**Sommerfeld model** (quantum): Fermi sea of non-interacting electrons.

**Fermi energy**: $E_F = \frac{\hbar^2}{2m}(3\pi^2n)^{2/3}$

**Fermi surface**: Boundary of filled states at T = 0.

---

## 4. Band Theory

**Bloch's theorem**: 
$$\psi_{n\vec{k}}(\vec{r}) = e^{i\vec{k}\cdot\vec{r}} u_n(\vec{r})$$

Wavefunction = plane wave × periodic part.

**Band structure** ε(k): Energy eigenvalues vs wave vector.

**Bandgap**: Energy gap between occupied (valence) and empty (conduction) bands.

- **Insulators**: Large bandgap (>5 eV)
- **Semiconductors**: Moderate bandgap (1-3 eV)
- **Metals**: No bandgap (valence band overlaps with conduction)

---

## 5. Semiconductors

**Intrinsic conduction**: Thermal excitation across bandgap.

**Extrinsic conduction**:
- **n-type** (donors): Add electrons (e.g., phosphorus in silicon)
- **p-type** (acceptors): Add holes (e.g., boron in silicon)

**Pn junction**: Interface creates electric field → diode, transistor, solar cell.

**Carrier concentration**:
$$n_i = \sqrt{N_c N_v} e^{-E_g/2k_B T}$$

---

## 6. Superconductivity

**Critical temperature** Tc: Below Tc, resistance drops to ZERO.

**BCS theory** (Cooper pairs): Electrons pair via phonon-mediated attraction.

**Meissner effect**: Magnetic field expelled from superconductor (diamagnetism).

**Energy gap**: At T = 0, gap Δ = 3.5 kBTc

**Applications**: MRI magnets, power transmission, quantum computing.

---

## 7. Phonons: Quantized Lattice Vibrations

**Acoustic branch**: Long-wavelength limit → sound waves, linear dispersion ω = vs|k|

**Optical branch**: Characteristic frequency ω₀ (raises/lowers dielectric constant)

**Debye model**: Approximates density of states via cutoff frequency ωD.

**Specific heat**: Approaches Cv = 3NkB (Dulong-Petit law) at high T.

---

## 8. Superconductivity and Phonons

Electron-phonon interaction fundamental to BCS superconductivity.

**Electron-phonon coupling**: Lattice distortion drags electron along.

**Cooper instability**: For any attractive interaction, pairing is energetically favorable.
