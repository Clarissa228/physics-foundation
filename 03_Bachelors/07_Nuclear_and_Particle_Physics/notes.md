# Nuclear and Particle Physics: Building Blocks of Matter

## Overview
Understanding the nucleus and fundamental particles: quarks, leptons, gauge bosons. From MeV (nuclear) to GeV (particle) energy scales. Standard Model is our best theory of fundamental physics.

---

## 1. Nuclear Properties

**Nucleus**: Protons (Z) + neutrons (N = A - Z), packed in volume ~10⁻¹⁵ m.

**Nuclear density**: ~2 × 10¹⁷ kg/m³ (neutron star density!)

**Nuclear binding**: Strong nuclear force (residual gluon exchange between quarks)

**Semi-empirical mass formula** (Bethe-Weizsäcker):
$$BE = a_v A - a_s A^{2/3} - a_c\frac{Z^2}{A^{1/3}} - a_a\frac{(A-2Z)^2}{A} + \delta(A,Z)$$

Terms: volume, surface, Coulomb repulsion, asymmetry, pairing.

---

## 2. Binding Energy Curve

**B.E./A vs A**: Peak at iron-56 (~8.8 MeV/nucleon).

**Consequences**:
- Nuclei lighter than Fe gain energy by fusing → fusion energy
- Nuclei heavier than Fe gain energy by splitting → fission energy
- This powers stars and nuclear reactors!

---

## 3. Nuclear Models

**Liquid drop model**: Nucleus as incompressible fluid drop (explains B.E. curve).

**Shell model**: Nucleons in shells like atomic electrons.
**Magic numbers**: 2, 8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126 (doubly magic nuclei especially stable)

---

## 4. Radioactive Decay

**Alpha decay** (α = ²₄He): Heavy nuclei (Z > 82)
$$^{238}_{92}U \to ^{234}_{90}Th + ^{4}_{2}He$$

**Beta decay** (β⁻ = electron): n → p + e⁻ + ν̄ₑ
**Beta-plus** (β⁺ = positron): p → n + e⁺ + νₑ
**Gamma decay** (γ = photon): Nucleus drops to lower energy state

**Decay law**: $N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t}$ where $\lambda = \ln 2 / t_{1/2}$

---

## 5. Fission and Fusion Energy

**Fission** (U-235 + thermal neutron):
$$^{235}U + n \to \text{fission fragments} + 2-3n + 200 \text{ MeV}$$

Chain reaction in reactor (controlled) or bomb (uncontrolled).

**Fusion** (deuterium + tritium):
$$^{2}H + ^{3}H \to ^{4}He + n + 17.6 \text{ MeV}$$

Powers stars, proposed for future fusion reactors.

---

## 6. Standard Model of Particle Physics

**Quarks** (spin-1/2):
- Up, down, charm, strange, top, bottom
- Confined in hadrons (never isolated)
- Fractional charge ±1/3, ±2/3

**Leptons** (spin-1/2):
- Electron, muon, tau
- Electron-neutrino, muon-neutrino, tau-neutrino
- No strong force interaction

**Gauge bosons** (force carriers):
- Photon γ: electromagnetism
- W±, Z⁰: weak force
- Gluons (8 types): strong force
- Higgs boson: gives mass to particles

---

## 7. Feynman Diagrams

Visualize particle interactions:
- Time flows up or right
- Particles: straight lines (fermions) or wavy (bosons)
- Vertices: interaction points

**Amplitude** ∝ coupling strength × diagram topology.

---

## 8. Fundamental Forces

1. **Strong** (color charge): ~10² (strongest at short range)
2. **Electromagnetic**: ~10⁻² (long range)
3. **Weak** (isospin): ~10⁻⁵ (very short range)
4. **Gravity**: ~10⁻⁴⁰ (weakest, not in Standard Model)

---

## 9. Particle Accelerators

**LHC** (Large Hadron Collider): 27 km ring, 14 TeV collision energy.

**Discovery**: Higgs boson (2012), confirms electroweak symmetry breaking.
