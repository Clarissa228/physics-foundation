# Electromagnetic Waves

## Overview
Maxwell's equations predict self-propagating electromagnetic waves that travel at speed c.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Maxwell's Equations
**Gauss's law**: $\oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{A} = Q/\varepsilon_0$
**No monopoles**: $\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{A} = 0$
**Faraday's law**: $\oint \vec{E} \cdot d\vec{l} = -d\Phi_B/dt$
**Ampere-Maxwell law**: $\oint \vec{B} \cdot d\vec{l} = \mu_0(I + \varepsilon_0 d\Phi_E/dt)$

### 2. Wave Equation
From Maxwell's equations: $\nabla^2 E = \mu_0\varepsilon_0 \frac{\partial^2 E}{\partial t^2}$

Speed of light: $c = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\mu_0\varepsilon_0}} = 3 \times 10^8$ m/s

### 3. EM Wave Properties
- Transverse waves (E, B perpendicular to propagation and to each other)
- E and B in phase, with $E = cB$
- Energy density: $u_E = u_B = \frac{1}{2}\varepsilon_0 E^2$

### 4. Poynting Vector
Energy flux: $\vec{S} = \frac{1}{\mu_0}(\vec{E} \times \vec{B})$

Magnitude: $S = \frac{E^2}{c\mu_0} = c\varepsilon_0 E^2$ (W/m²)

Average (intensity): $I = \frac{1}{2}c\varepsilon_0 E_0^2 = \frac{E_0^2}{2Z_0}$ where $Z_0 = \sqrt{\mu_0/\varepsilon_0} = 377Ω$

### 5. Radiation Pressure
Momentum transfer from EM waves: $p = U/c$

Pressure: $P = \frac{I}{c}$

### 6. Electromagnetic Spectrum
- Radio: λ > 1 mm, f < 300 GHz
- Microwave: 1mm-1μm
- Infrared: 1μm-700nm
- Visible: 700nm(red)-400nm(violet)
- Ultraviolet: 400nm-10nm
- X-rays: 10nm-0.01nm
- Gamma rays: < 0.01nm

### 7. Wave Superposition
Two waves: $E = E_1\cos(\omega t - kx) + E_2\cos(\omega t - kx + \phi)$

Resultant amplitude: $E_R = \sqrt{E_1^2 + E_2^2 + 2E_1E_2\cos\phi}$

**Constructive interference**: φ = 0, 2π, ... → $E_R = E_1 + E_2$
**Destructive interference**: φ = π, 3π, ... → $E_R = |E_1 - E_2|$

## Historical Context
- **Maxwell (1865)**: Unified electricity and magnetism
- **Hertz (1887)**: First experimental generation and detection
- **Marconi (1890s)**: Radio transmission
- **Hertzsprung-Russell diagram**: Classification of EM emission
