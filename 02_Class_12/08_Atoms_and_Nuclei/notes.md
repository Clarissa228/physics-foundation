# Atoms and Nuclei

## Overview
Atomic structure from Bohr model; nuclear physics covering composition, energy, and decay.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Rutherford Scattering
Nuclear radius: $r \approx R_0 A^{1/3}$ where $R_0 \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-15}$ m

Impact parameter: $b = \frac{kZ_1Z_2e^2}{2KE}\cot(\theta/2)$

### 2. Bohr Model
Radius: $r_n = n^2 a_0$ where $a_0 = 0.529$ Å (Bohr radius)

Energy: $E_n = -\frac{13.6}{n^2}$ eV

Transition: $\Delta E = h\nu = 13.6(\frac{1}{n_f^2} - \frac{1}{n_i^2})$ eV

### 3. Hydrogen Spectrum Series
- **Lyman**: n_i → 1 (UV, Δν = 13.6(1 - 1/n²))
- **Balmer**: n_i → 2 (visible)
- **Paschen**: n_i → 3 (IR)
- **Brackett**: n_i → 4 (far IR)
- **Pfund**: n_i → 5 (far IR)

### 4. Nuclear Composition
Nucleus = Z protons + N neutrons
Mass number: A = Z + N
Atomic number: Z = number of protons

**Isotopes**: Same Z, different A

### 5. Nuclear Mass and Binding Energy
Mass defect: Δm = Zm_p + Nm_n - M_nucleus

Binding energy: $BE = \Delta m c^2 = 931.5 \text{ MeV (in AMU)}$

Binding energy per nucleon: BE/A (measures stability)

### 6. Radioactive Decay
Decay law: $N(t) = N_0 e^{-\lambda t} = N_0(1/2)^{t/t_{1/2}}$

Activity: $A = \lambda N$ (Becquerel or Curie)

**Alpha decay**: ^A_Z X → ^(A-4)_(Z-2) Y + ^4_2 He

**Beta decay**: ^A_Z X → ^A_(Z+1) Y + e^- + ν_e

**Gamma decay**: Nucleus transitions from excited to ground state

### 7. Half-life
Time for N to decrease to N_0/2: $t_{1/2} = \frac{\ln 2}{\lambda} = 0.693/\lambda$

### 8. Fission and Fusion
**Fission**: Heavy nucleus splits (e.g., U-235)
**Fusion**: Light nuclei combine (e.g., H-2 + H-3 → He-4 + n)

Both release energy because middle-A nuclei are most stable.

### 9. Binding Energy Curve
- Light nuclei: binding energy increases with A (fusion releases energy)
- Heavy nuclei: binding energy decreases with A (fission releases energy)
- Maximum at A ≈ 56 (Fe-56)

### 10. Q-value
Energy released in nuclear reaction: Q = (M_initial - M_final)c²

Q > 0: exothermic (energy released)
Q < 0: endothermic (energy absorbed)

## Historical Context
- **Rutherford (1911)**: Nuclear model
- **Bohr (1913)**: Atomic model with quantization
- **Chadwick (1932)**: Neutron discovery
- **Fermi (1942)**: First nuclear reactor
- **Curie (1903)**: Radioactivity (Nobel Prize)
