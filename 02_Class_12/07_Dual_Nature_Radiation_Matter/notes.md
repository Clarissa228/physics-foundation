# Dual Nature of Radiation and Matter

## Overview
Light exhibits both wave and particle (photon) behavior; matter exhibits both particle and wave behavior.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Photoelectric Effect
Einstein's explanation: $h\nu = \phi + KE_{max}$

where:
- $h\nu$ = photon energy
- $\phi$ = work function
- $KE_{max}$ = maximum kinetic energy of electrons

**Threshold frequency**: $\nu_0 = \phi/h$

**Stopping potential**: $V_s = KE_{max}/e$

### 2. Planck's Quantum Hypothesis
Energy of photon: $E = h\nu = \hbar\omega$

where:
- $h = 6.626 \times 10^{-34}$ J⋅s (Planck constant)
- $\hbar = h/(2\pi)$

### 3. de Broglie Wavelength
Momentum of photon: $p = E/c = h/\lambda$

For any particle: $\lambda = \frac{h}{p} = \frac{h}{mv}$

### 4. Davisson-Germer Experiment
Electron diffraction confirms matter has wavelike properties.

Bragg's condition: $2d\sin\theta = n\lambda$

### 5. Heisenberg Uncertainty Principle
$$\Delta x \cdot \Delta p \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}$$

$$\Delta E \cdot \Delta t \geq \frac{\hbar}{2}$$

**Implication**: Cannot simultaneously know position and momentum precisely.

### 6. Wave-Particle Duality
- **Photons**: Particle in emission/absorption, wave in propagation
- **Electrons**: Particle in measurement, wave between measurements

### 7. Blackbody Radiation
**Planck distribution**: $u(\nu)d\nu = \frac{8\pi h\nu^3}{c^3} \frac{d\nu}{e^{h\nu/k_BT} - 1}$

**Wien's displacement law**: $\lambda_{max}T = 2.898 \times 10^{-3}$ m⋅K

**Stefan-Boltzmann law**: $P = \sigma AT^4$

### 8. Compton Scattering
Wavelength shift: $\Delta\lambda = \lambda_C(1 - \cos\theta)$

where $\lambda_C = h/(m_e c) = 2.43 \times 10^{-12}$ m (Compton wavelength)

### 9. Pair Production and Annihilation
Photon → electron + positron: requires $E > 2m_e c^2 = 1.022$ MeV

Electron + positron → photons: releases $2m_e c^2$ energy

## Historical Context
